⏱️ Part 2: A Wild Animal

Describe a wild animal that you want to learn more about.

You should say:

  • What it is
  • When/where you saw it (or heard about it)
  • Why you want to learn more about it
  • And explain what you want to learn more about it

🔑 Recommended Vocabulary (Vietnamese Context)

The Saola Kỳ lân Châu Á (rất hiếm ở VN)
Endangered Có nguy cơ tuyệt chủng
Habitat Môi trường sống tự nhiên
Conservation Sự bảo tồn

📝 Sample Monologue (Band 6.5)

[Intro & What it is]
"I would like to talk about the Saola, which is often called the 'Asian Unicorn'. It is a very rare and fascinating wild animal that is native to the forests of Vietnam and Laos."

[When/where you saw it]
"Obviously, I have never seen one in real life because they are extremely rare. In fact, no biologist has seen them in the wild for years. I first heard about the Saola when I was watching a nature documentary on VTV about endangered species in the Annamite Range (dãy Trường Sơn). Later, I also saw pictures of it in a biology textbook. It looks a bit like an antelope with two long, straight horns."

[Why you want to learn more about it]
"There are a couple of reasons why I want to know more about this animal. Firstly, it represents the rich biodiversity of my country, Vietnam. It is a symbol of our nature. Secondly, it is shrouded in mystery. Because it hides so well in the deep jungle, scientists actually know very little about its lifestyle, its diet, or how many are left in the world."

[What you want to learn]
"If I had the chance, I would love to learn about their natural habitat and how they survive. More importantly, I want to learn about the conservation efforts being made to protect them. I know they are facing huge threats from illegal hunting and deforestation. I want to know what ordinary people or the government can do to prevent the Saola from going completely extinct. That is the animal I’m most curious about."

🗣️ Part 3: Animals, Zoos & Conservation

🔑 Key Vocabulary

Companionship Sự bầu bạn (của thú cưng)
Poaching Săn bắt động vật trái phép
Ecosystem balance Sự cân bằng hệ sinh thái
Educational purpose Mục đích giáo dục (của sở thú)

Band 6.5 Answer: "The main advantage is companionship. Pets like dogs or cats can reduce stress and make people feel less lonely. However, the disadvantage is that keeping a pet requires a lot of time and money for food, vaccines, and cleaning. It's a big responsibility."

Band 6.5 Answer: "Yes, Vietnam has a few endemic species. As I mentioned earlier, the Saola is a very famous one. Another example is the Golden-headed Langur (Voọc đầu vàng), a type of monkey found only in Cat Ba Island. They are completely unique to our nature."

Band 6.5 Answer: "I think attitudes are changing for the better. The younger generation in Vietnam is very aware of animal rights and conservation. However, some older people still believe in using wild animal parts for traditional medicine, which unfortunately encourages poaching."

Band 6.5 Answer: "The biggest benefit is maintaining the ecosystem balance. Every animal plays a role in nature. If predators die out, other animals might overpopulate and destroy forests. Also, protecting wildlife ensures that our future children can see these animals in real life, not just in history books."

Band 6.5 Answer: "I believe zoos serve a strong educational purpose. They allow children living in cities to see animals they would never see otherwise, which teaches them to love nature. Furthermore, good zoos act as breeding centers to help save endangered species from going extinct."
