Dynamic Charts

Line graphs, Tables, Bar charts, Pie Charts showing changes over time.

Source References

Number of tourists visiting a Caribbean island (2010–2017) Line Graph
Export Earnings (2015–2016) & Percentage Change Table Bar Chart & Table

Essay Structure

1. Introduction

  • The line graph provides a breakdown of + Noun/ Noun phrase in … (Meanwhile, the line graph illustrates…)
  • The graph compares … in terms of + Noun/ Noun phrase
  • between … and … / from … to … / in the … to … period

2. Overview

  • Looking from an overall perspective, a noticeable feature is that while … follows an upward trend, the reverse is true for …
  • Looking from an overall perspective, a noticeable feature is that while … follows an upward trend, … remains relatively stable.
  • What is more, … is always/consistently/invariably the most substantial / modest.
  • Notably, … a positive/negative correlation between … and …

3. Body 1

  • Commencing at 1 million visitors, the number/proportion of … increases … before …
  • This is followed by …

4. Body 2

  • In contrast, there is a general diminution in …

Fluctuation

Small / Slow Changes
  • Modest / slight (a): nhẹ, ít
  • Negligible (a): rất ít
  • Progressive / Steady / gradual: đều đặn, từ từ
Large / Fast Changes
  • Substantial / Significant / Sharp: mạnh, nhiều
  • Steep / considerable / remarkable: đáng kể, dốc
  • With the exception of / except (for)
  • The reverse is true for / The reverse is the case for
  • Comparable (a): similar
  • Range … from … (v): nằm trong khoảng
  • This is followed by + a/an + adj + noun
  • There is a substantial diminution in …
  • Nouns for numbers: The figure / the statistic / the data / the value
  • Differences: Gap / difference / discrepancy
  • Future trends: Be projected / anticipated / predicted to
  • The year 2020 witnessed a modest rise in …
  • Multipliers: (to be) double / triple / quadruple / five times higher than
  • Rank second / third
  • The former / the latter

    • Well below

Essay Structure

Maps

Describing town plans, building layouts, and how locations change over time.

1. Introduction

The maps illustrate the layout / blueprint of + [Name of the Place] in [Year 1] and how it looks/looked/will look in [Year 2].

Synonyms for look: alter, transform

2. Overview

Looking from an overall perspective, it is apparent/noticeable that [Name of the Place] undergoes / underwent / has undergone / will undergo a number of substantial transformations / alterations, with…

  • an increase / a decrease in …
  • the conversion of … into
  • the addition / expansion of …
  • facility / amenity / infrastructure
  • green space
Example: "Looking from an overall perspective, it is noticeable that Stokeford will undergo a number of substantial transformations, with an increase in infrastructures/facilities/amenities and accommodation and a decrease in green spaces."

Useful Structures for Maps

Describing the PRESENT Map

1. Introducing Layout
  • At present, the area consists of…
  • Currently, the site features…
  • The existing layout shows…
2. Locations
  • in the northern/southern/eastern/western area
  • in the northeast/southwest corner
  • along the main road / beside the river
  • in the centre of the site
3. Useful Verbs
  • There is / There are…
  • A footpath runs through…
  • A car park occupies…
  • The buildings are situated…
4. Relationships
adjacent to / next to surrounded by located between X and Y opposite the entrance

Describing the FUTURE Map

Use "will" + passive for planned changes
1. Introducing developments
  • According to the future plan, the area will be redeveloped into…
  • Several major changes will take place in the coming years.
  • The site is expected to undergo significant transformation.
  • By the time the redevelopment is complete, the area will have been modernised.
2. Additions
  • A new shopping centre will be built in the northern area.
  • A cycle path will be constructed alongside the road.
  • Additional housing will be added in the east.
  • A sports complex is planned to be built in the southwest.
3. Removals
  • The current car park will be demolished.
  • The woodland area will be cleared to make room for…
  • The existing shops will be replaced by…
4. Replacements & Upgrades
  • The old café will be converted into a restaurant.
  • The playground will be replaced with an indoor activity centre.
  • The farmland will be redeveloped into residential buildings.
  • The main road will be extended to connect to the new entrance.
  • The park area will be enlarged to include more green space.
  • The car park will be expanded to accommodate additional vehicles.
5. Unchanged & Linking
  • Despite the planned developments, the river will remain unchanged.
  • The central square will be retained. / The existing footpath will be preserved.
  • Meanwhile, the southern section will feature…
  • In contrast, the northern area will undergo more extensive changes.
  • Additionally, the eastern side will be redesigned to include…

Process / Diagrams

Describing manufacturing processes, natural cycles, or system workflows.

Essay Structure

1. Introduction & 2. Overview

  • The diagram illustrates the steps involved in the procedure of manufacturing/producing …
  • Looking from an overall perspective, the process comprises a total of [number] distinct stages, commencing with [starting point] and progressing through a sequence of steps before culminating in [final stage/outcome].

3. Body 1 (Initial Steps)

  • The process begins when S+V.
    Ex: The process begins when the raw materials are gathered.
  • This is followed by + N/NP.
  • The next/following step involves + V-ing/NP.
    Ex: The next step involves the extraction of raw materials.

4. Body 2 (Subsequent Steps)

  • The process proceeds with + N/NP.
    Ex: The procedure proceeds with the cooling of the material.
  • Once the […] has been completed, […] takes place.
  • Following this, S + V.
  • Subsequent to this / Subsequently, …
    Ex: Subsequently, the material is compressed into blocks.

Useful Vocab & Phrases

Proceed with sth (v) tiếp tục với
Be transferred to [machine/place] where S+V
Ex: The mixture is transferred to a furnace where it is melted.
Be subjected to sth bị chịu / bị đưa vào / bị tác động bởi điều gì đó
Ex: The water is subjected to a filtration process.
Undergo (v) trải qua
Ex: Subsequently, the combination undergoes a freezing process.
