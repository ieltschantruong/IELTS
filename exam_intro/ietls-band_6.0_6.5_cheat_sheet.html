IELTS Academic Exam

Intensive Preparation Cheat Sheet Computer-Delivered

Target Score Band 6.0 - 6.5
Duration ~2 Hours 45 Mins
Goal Consistent Basics + Low Errors

1. Test Structure & Time Allocation

Listening

30m

  • • 4 Parts (40 Questions)
  • • Social & Academic Contexts
  • • Dialogues & Monologues
  • • No 10-min transfer time! (Only 2 mins to check)

Reading

60m

  • • 3 Passages (40 Questions)
  • • Academic Texts
  • • Books, Journals, Magazines
  • • Screen split: text left, questions right. Highlight tools on screen.

Writing

60m

  • • Task 1 (20m): Graph/Map/Process description (150+ words)
  • • Task 2 (40m): Formal essay (250+ words)
  • • Auto word-count & Copy/Paste enabled

Speaking

11-14m

  • • Part 1: Intro & Familiar Topics
  • • Part 2: Long Turn (1m prep, 2m speak)
  • • Part 3: Abstract Discussion
  • • In-person or via video call at test center

2. Objective Scoring (Listening & Reading)

Band 6.0 ~23-26 / 40
Band 6.5 ~27-29 / 40
Strategy: You don't need to be perfect. You can miss 11-17 questions. Secure the "easy" points in Listening Parts 1 & 2, and Reading Passage 1.

3. Subjective Scoring (Writing & Speaking)

Requirements for Band 6.0-6.5

Task Achievement / Response

Clear overview in Task 1. All prompt parts addressed in Task 2. Maintain a clear position.

Coherence & Cohesion / Fluency

Logical paragraphing. Willing to speak at length. Uses basic cohesive devices effectively.

Lexical Resource (Vocabulary)

Adequate range. Attempts less common words, meaning is clear despite some inaccuracies.

Grammatical Range & Pronunciation

Mix of simple & complex sentences. Errors exist but rarely impede communication. Easily understood.

4. Core Skill Dynamics

Receptive vs. Productive

Reading & Listening (Receptive): Require strict time-management, scanning, and predicting.

Writing & Speaking (Productive): Require active language generation, quick ideation, and structural planning under pressure.

Contextual Shifts

Listening: Blends everyday social situations with academic lectures.

Reading & Writing: Strictly academic and formal.

Speaking: Conversational and personal, even when discussing abstract concepts.
